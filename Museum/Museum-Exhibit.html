Ancient History: The Pyramids of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex bordering present-day Giza in Greater Cairo, Egypt. It is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

Inventions: The Printing Press

Invented by Johannes Gutenberg around 1440, the movable type printing press introduced the era of mass communication. It permanently altered the structure of society by making knowledge accessible to the public.

‚ö° Quick Facts

  • The first major book printed was the "Gutenberg Bible".
  • It increased the speed of book production exponentially.
  • It helped fuel the Renaissance and the Scientific Revolution.

Famous People: Ada Lovelace

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace, was an English mathematician and writer. She is chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

‚ö° Quick Facts

  • She is widely considered the first computer programmer.
  • She realized computers could do more than just calculate numbers.
  • She was the daughter of the poet Lord Byron.

Artifact: The Rosetta Stone

The Rosetta Stone is a granodiorite stele inscribed with three versions of a decree issued in Memphis, Egypt, in 196 BC. Because it included Ancient Greek, it was the key to deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphs.

‚ö° Quick Facts

  • Discovered by French soldiers in 1799.
  • It contains the same text in Hieroglyphs, Demotic, and Greek.
  • Currently housed in the British Museum in London.
